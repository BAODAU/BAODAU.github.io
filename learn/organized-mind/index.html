  • flow state: different state of being

  • during flow, two key regions of the brai ndeactivate: the portion of the prefrontal cortex responsible for self-criticism, and the amygdala, the brain’s fear center.

    flow state

  • attention is focused on a limited perceptual field, and that field receives your full concentration and complete investment

  • action and awareness merge.you cease thinking about yourself as separate from the activity or the world, and you don’t think of your actions and your perceptions as being distinct: what you think becomes what you do.

  • during flow, you experience freedom from worry about failure; you are aware of what needs to be done, but you don’t feel that you are doing it. the ego is not involved and falls away completely.

  • flow is a lack of distractibility: the same old distractions are there, but we’re not tempted to attend to them.

  • we monitor our performance without the kinds of self-defeating negative judgments

  • flow states don’t occur for just any old task or activity. they can occur only when one is deply focused on the task, when the task requires intense concentration and commitment, provides immediate feedback, and is perfectly matched to one’s skill level

  • if the task you are engaged in is too simple, holding no challenge, i’ll get bored. that boredom will break my attention to the task, and my mind will wander into the default mode. if the task is too difficult and holds too many challenges. i’ll become frustrated and experience anxiety. the greater my skill is, the greater the opportunity to achieve flow.

  • the flow state is characterized by a total lack of conscious awareness, am erging of your self with the project itself, a seamless melding of thought, action, movment, and result.

  • the higher your skill level, the more easily you can practive those skills automatically, subconsciously, and then the more easily you can disengage your conscioys mind, your ego, and other enemies of flow.

  • easier for those who have invested a great deal of time to a given dopamin

what flow means

  • high level of engagement. info access and processing seem effortless. skills we didn’t know we had emerges. with no need to exercise self-control to stay focused, we free neural resources to the task at hand.
  • during flow states, we no longer need to exert ourselves to stay on task: it happens automatically as we enter this specialized attentional state.
  • it takes less energy to be in flow than to be distracted. this is why flow states are periods of great productivity and efficiency
  • flow is a chemically different state. flow occurs when you are not explicitly thinking about what you’re doing. your brain is in a special mode of activity in which procedures and operations are performed automatically without your having to exert conscious control.
  • trying to think about what you’re doing can quickly interferem ending the automaticity and high performance level you’ve enjoyed.
