Models and Patterns#

Include linear equations, rules, clusters, graphs, tree structures, and recurrent patterns in time series.

The data sets examined are often large.

Process#

  1. determining the nature and structure of the representation
  2. deciding how to quantify and compare how well different representations fit the data
  3. choose an algorithmic process to optimize the score function
  4. deciding what principples of data management are required to implement the algoirthms efficiently

Regression analysis#

In the form $Y = aX + B$, with X as a predictor variable and Y as a response variable.

In this situation, the score function is the sum of squared discrepancies between the predicted value and the observed value. $$ \sum_{i=1}^{n}(X_i-\bar{X})^2 $$ with $n$ being the number of observations.
