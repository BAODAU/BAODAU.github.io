Greene Power

January 10, 2021 · 4 min · baoqdau

The moment you lose contact with your people, seeking security in isolation, rebellion is brewing.

Never think you’re so elevated that you can afford to cut yourself off from even the lowest echelons.

Social arts that make us pleasant to be around can be practiced only by constant exposure and circulation. The more you are in contact with others, the more graceful and at ease you become.

Isolation engenders an awkwardness in your gestures and leads to further isolation, as people start avoiding you.

The effects of isolation on the human mind: a loss of proportion, an obsession with detail combined with an inability to see the larger picture.

Since power is a human creation, it is increased by contact with other people. You need to be permeable, able to float in and out of different circles and mix with different types. That kind of mobility and social contact will protect you from plotters who will be unable to keep secrets from you and enemies who will be unable to isolate you from your allies. Always on the move, you mix and mingle in the rooms of the palace, never sitting or settling in one place.

It is hardly ever right to choose isolation. Without keeping an ear on what is happening in the streets you will be unable to protect yourself.

The lack of distance from other people can make it impossible to think clearly about what is going on around you. The more isolated you are, the harder it is to break out of your isolation when you choose to.

Be careful to keep your way back into society open.

Do not offend the wrong person#

Be convinced that there are no persons so insignificant and inconsiderable. Wrongs are often forgiven, but contempt never wears off.

Measure people and know you’re dealing with. Don’t offend the wrong people, don’t choose the wrong types to work on, and will think you are flattering people when you are actually insulting them. Study people’s weaknesses, the chinks in their armor, their areas of both pride and insecurity. Know their ins and outs before you even decide whether or not to deal with them.

Never rely on your instincts. Learn to see through appearances and their contradictions.

Learn to tell the lions from the lambs or pay the price.

Do not commit to anyone#

Do not commit to any side or cause but yourself. by maintaining your independence, you become the master of others: playing people against one another, making them pursue you.

Refusing to commit to a person or group will enhance your image. You seem powerful because you make yourself ungraspable. As your reputation for independence grows, more people will come to desire your, wanting to be the one who gets you to commit.

Desire is like a virus: If we see that someone is desired by other people, we tend to find this person desirable to.

Put yourself in the middle between competing powers. Lure one side with the promise of your help. The other side will pursue you as well.

Keep yourself free of commitments and obligations: they are the device of others to get you into their power.

Play a sucker to catch a sucker#

Make your victims feel smart, smarter than you are. Once convinced, they will never suspect that you have ulterior motives.

Be the perfect courtier#

Be a source of pleasure#

Not everyone can play the role of favorite, for not everyone is blessed with charm and wit. but we can all control our unpleasant qualities and obscure them when necessary.

A man who knows the court is master of his gestures, of his eyes and of his face; he is profound, impenetrable; he dissimulates bad offices, smiles at his enemies, controls his irritation, disguises his passions, belies his heart, speaks and acts against his feelings.
