being busy#

There are times in most of our lives when being busy is a refuge. By keeping ourselves busy, we avoid finding the quiet time needed reflect on difficult choices. We know that we are in a situation that needs to change and yet, we create busyness to avoid making a hard decision.

Life is not fair#

  • there are factors and events beyond your control that will impact how your life plays out.
  • by putting yourself in a position to succeed, you maximize the chances you will achieve whatever it is that you want.

Review of how I’m spending my Time#

Awareness#

  • an awareness of how you experience time can tell you a lot about yourself
  • Objective: balance your consumption of time in a way that meshes goals and passions.
  • keeping a journal sensitizes you as to how you are spending your depreciating asset: the hours of your life
  • if you do not have an awareness of how you are spending time, you have no ability to manage your time effectively

Goals#

What are your goals?

What do you hope to accomplish in the short term?

What do you want your life to look like in the long term?

How do you plan on getting from where you are to where you want to be?

What are the steps from here to there?

Goals provide the road map#

  • By thinking about steps and time, you engage your analytical and creative juices. You have a better understanding of what you have to do to get where you want to be.

  • You are starting to plan. You are starting to actually visualize the achievment of your goals. Visualization increases desire which increases intensity.

  • You are testing your resolve. By identifying specific steps and time requirements, you double check your intensity for certain goals.

Choices#

  • You need to find a balance between living for the moment: doing exactly what you want to do right now. And living for the future: putting yourself in a position to lead the life you want at some point down the road.
  • time management is in large part about choices. calls from friends. the desire to sleep late. commitments and obligations. seduction of the internet.

Successful people do not enjoy doing the things that others do not. Successful people simply do them nevertheless.

  • by constantly reflecting on your goals and readjusting at times,you increase the probability that your life will proceed on your agenda, and not someone else’s or by fate.

The secret of success is learning how to use pain and pleasure instead of having pain and pleasure use you. If you do that, you’re in control of your life. If you don’t, life controls you.

Using the hours you have to maximum effectiveness#

  • not all hours are created equal
  • no magic: exercise daily, eat healthy, get lots of fluids, moderate your intake of caffeine and alcohol.
  • use the time when you have the most energy. The power of Full engagement

create time#

  • downtime periods: times when you are waiting for something to happen and when you you are cooling your heels.

minimize time waste#

  • make decisions quickly
  • speed reading
  • improve memorization skills

improve memorization skills#

  • stretch your mind: do activities that forces you to push yourself mentally
  • spread out your intake of food and drink: when you overload yourself with food and/or drink, your sugar level spikes. and then shortly drops dramatically.
  • exercise: increases blood flow to the brain
  • antioxidants: berries, broccoli, spinach.
  • learn memory skills,
  • perform associations/rhymes
  • get enough sleep
  • concentrate
  • convert important facts into a format that assists your recall.
  • dont stress about memory skills

procrastination#

  • result of a powerful law: INERTIA
  • in order to achieve forward motion, people need to push themselves

strategies to defeat procrastination#

  • keep a piece of paper or index card in your pocket with your goals written in bold ink:
  • break big projects up into manageable pieces: most of us are overwhelmed when contemplating a huge project.
  • train your mind to recognize the first signs of laziness or procrastination:
  • always carry a means of taking notes: putting your thoughts in writing will help propel you into action the next chance you have to get started

use INERTIA to your advantage#

  • people who develop the mental strength and fortitude to deal with procrastination and the other challenges we all face are most likely to achieve their dreams

  • those who develop mental strength are more likely to:

          1. keep on schedule/plan
      2. make appropriate choices
      3. maintain good focus
      4. defeat procrastination

develop strong mental fortitude#

  • you are not your mind. you have dominion over negative thoughts in your head: read The power of now

The single most important step you can take is to learn to disidentify from you mind. At some point you will smile at the thoughts in your mind, as if they are antics of a small child.

Eckhart Tolle The power of now
  • thoughts are things just like any other tangible item
  • your brain can only process one dominate thought at a time: when you feel a thought creeping into your mind, train yourself to think of something else: a strong positive image or idea

Clutter#

the enemy of productivity#

  • the part of your brain that holds all of the incomplete, undecided and unorganized stuff has limited capacity. It’s very easy to get distracted.
  • you want a mind like water

Effective time management#

make your work area attractive#

  • equip it with everything you need to work:
  • you should enjoy spending time in your work area.
  • Everything you want and need should be near at hand.

Create a filing system#

  • putting all material relevant to a subject in one place is a huge step to better time management and organization

ONE LIST#

  • that you use to write or type EVERYTHING that requires your attention.

move every item#

  • as much as possible, every piece of paper, email, text or voice message should be acted on

  • anything that you can do in two minutes or less, do it now

  • make a decision to

          1. act on it immediately
      2. toss or delete it 
      3. put it in your ONE LIST

convert thoughts to action steps#

  • no more ‘consider’, ‘analyze’, or ‘think about’.
  • Put down exactly what you’ll do: read about, speak to, make decision about.

maintain a good tickler system#

  • any reliable method that reminds you what you need to do and when

review weekly your systems for retention and productivity#

  • are your systems working as well as they should?

revisit long-term goals at least once a month#

  • are you making the right choices on a regular basis to move yourself close to your goals.

Planning#

  • you should spend at least 10 minutes to plan each of your days
  • by taking just ten minutes to reflect on what it is that you want to accomplish and how to do so. you will improve the odds that what you want to happen will happen
  • by thinking ahead, you are likely to reduce the effort you need to complete whatever task in in front of you.

Questions to think as you plan#

  • What is it that I want to accomplish today?
  • How much time do I have to do that?
  • Can I defer doing some items which are not important to another day?
  • Is there a logical order to doing what I need to get done

todo list#

why to do lists work#

organizing your time#

  • by forcing yourself to make a todo list, you are organizing your time. You are thinking through all that you need to get done and writing it down. By thinking about what needs to be done and what doesn’t, you create a framework for your day.

reduce stress#

  • having a todo list reduces your stress. When you organize your days, your stress maybe reduced just by seeing your day visually

daily exercise in goal setting and choice making#

  • requires you to rethink how you are spending your time and why

fun#

  • it’s like writing a mini-novel with the main character being you.
  • what you list is an act of creative Planning

Prioritizing#

  • success is not an accident. Success is the result of repeatedly doing those things that optimize the probability of a favorable outcome

habits#

  • too many people prioritize by reaction - doing what is pressing- rather than by proaction doing what is important.
  • be careful that you are not being busy in place of effective.

prioritization quadrants#

Important and UrgentImportant, but not Urgent
Urgent, but not Importantnot Urgent and not Important
  • successful time management requires proaction.
  • On How to take control of your time and life, Alan Lakein suggested that we start the day contemplating what we need to get done and rank them from A to C.

  • always tackle the hardest project first
  • the squeaky wheel gets the grease. It means that the person or chore that makes the most immediate or noisiest demand gets attention.

streamlining#

  • the best way to accomplish a goal is to work exclusively at that goal until you complete

In the strictest sense you shouldn’t be trying to do more in each day, trying to fill every second with a work fidget of some type. It took me a long time to figure this out. I used to be very fond of the results-by-volume approach.

Tim Ferris Four hour work week

incorporate the 80-20 rule into our lives#

  • define 1 goal.
  • List 5 things that I’m doing to achieve that goal
  • which of those 5 things I’m doing is most likely to help
  • that’s the thing to give most energy and attention to

focus#

  • more focus by fewer distractions
  • you can’t multitask

techniques#

  • learn to discard quickly and without guilt
  • make an immediate decision what to do
  • put every piece of paper, book or article that pertains to a single project in one place
  • pick two times a day to answer voice messages, texts or emails
  • wear a watch
  • keep handy some mechanism for taking down ideas that pop into my head during the day
  • speed reading
  • ask for help
  • finish a task in one sitting or segment of time
  • move on

speed reading#

  • read first sentence of every paragraph
  • read all words in bold, underlined or italicized
  • read just the introductory and conclusion chapters or paragraphs
  • scan vertically, not horizontally
  • no mouthing
  • don’t reread until finish a page or several paragraphs, only if you think you missed something

Conclusion#

  • self-awareness is the sine qua non. keep a time journal for a week or so. watch for areas where you might re-allocate your time expenditures.
  • goal-setting is the first step: there is no point to worrying about the passage of time unless and until you have identified those goals or objectives you are trying to achieve
  • choices are tough: ultimately about making choices.
  • you can create time by turning unproductive time into productive time. Increase your energy with exercise and healthy eating. The more energy you bring to an hour, the more powerful that hour becomes in moving you toward the realization of your goals.
  • procrastination is the enemy
  • aim for ‘a mind like water’. Declutter your work area, your life and eventually your mind, you increase your effectiveness dramatically.
  • planning and preparation are worth the effort
  • prioritize: separate necessary from accessory
  • single-minded focus: distractions are dream killers. They dilute your braininess. When you decide to work, then work, and do not let interruptions from third parties interfere with the effort.
  • pick and choose the time management techniques that work for you.
